An international initiative to develop a core outcome set for leptomeningeal disease

LeptoCOS is a multidisciplinary, international initiative developing a core outcome set for leptomeningeal disease (LMD): an agreed minimum set of outcomes every trial should measure and report. This companion presents the evidence base for that consensus, and where the initiative now stands.

Background

Why leptomeningeal disease needs a core outcome set

Leptomeningeal disease is a late stage of metastatic cancer, poor-prognosis and rising in incidence. Trials are small and scattered over three decades. When they do not share an outcome language, their results cannot be pooled, compared, or graded for guidelines. A core outcome set is the agreed minimum set of outcomes every trial should report: an agreed minimum, not a limit on what else may be reported.

Finding 1 · inconsistent definitions

Leptomeningeal treatment response is defined inconsistently

Agreeing that an outcome matters is not enough. The most-reported response outcome is defined many different ways; survival is timed inconsistently; and most outcomes name no assessor.

Reported by 21 of 41 trials; only 6 used a RANO-LM based standard (5 RANO-LM, 1 modified); the other 15 were study-specific.

Reference

Response-assessment criteria for leptomeningeal disease

The response-assessment frameworks behind the leptomeningeal-response definitions used in this review, drawn from published criteria. Study-specific composites combine two or more of these elements in a trial-defined way.

Response-assessment definitions

RANO-LM: Response Assessment in Neuro-Oncology, Leptomeningeal MetastasesComposite response combining a standardized neurological examination, CSF cytology (or flow cytometry in haematological cancers), and contrast-enhanced neuraxis MRI graded on a radiological LM response scorecard. Chamberlain et al., Neuro-Oncology 2017.
Modified RANO-LM (radiographic only)The radiographic (MRI) component of RANO-LM applied on its own, without the neurological-examination or CSF-cytology components.
CSF cytology conversionConversion of cerebrospinal-fluid cytology from positive (malignant cells present) to negative (no malignant or equivocal cells). EANO-ESMO Clinical Practice Guideline, ESMO Open 2023.
RECIST 1.1 (LM as non-measurable disease)Leptomeningeal disease is listed under RECIST 1.1 as truly non-measurable, so it is tracked qualitatively rather than as a measurable target lesion. Eisenhauer et al., 2009.

Transparency

Methods & limitations

Outcomes were extracted from published leptomeningeal disease trials following COMET, COS-STAD and COS-STAP, standardised to defined terms and mapped to COMET core areas. This is a descriptive catalogue of reported outcomes, not the final core outcome set.

The ask

An invitation to the Society for Neuro-Oncology

LeptoCOS invites the Society for Neuro-Oncology to support the initiative and help disseminate the resulting core outcome set to its members. A two-round eDelphi across all stakeholder groups will prioritise this long list into the core set; this companion presents the evidence, it does not collect votes. SNO’s engagement brings a usable standard for a high-mortality, under-served disease, a hand in setting it with members and patients at the consensus table before the set is fixed, and continuity with COSMIC, the meningioma core outcome set built by the same COMET method.
