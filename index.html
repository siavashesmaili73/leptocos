Project summary

An international initiative to develop a core outcome set for leptomeningeal disease

LeptoCOS is a multidisciplinary, international initiative developing a core outcome set for leptomeningeal disease (LMD): an agreed minimum set of outcomes every trial should measure and report. This companion presents the evidence base for that consensus and shows where the initiative now stands.

Registered as COMET Initiative Study 3224 · conducted in accordance with COMET, COS-STAD and PRISMA.

41 included studies1991 to 2025 316 reported outcomes55 standardised outcomes 24 sub-domains5 COMET core areas

Background

Why leptomeningeal disease needs a core outcome set

Leptomeningeal disease is a late-stage complication of cancer. It carries a poor prognosis and is rising in incidence. Trials are small and scattered over three decades. When they do not share an outcome language, their results cannot readily be pooled, compared, or graded for guidelines. A core outcome set is the agreed minimum set of outcomes every trial should measure and report, not a limit on what else may be reported.

Fragmented reporting

316 outcomes reduce to 55 standardised outcomes; only 3 are reported by at least half of the trials.

Priority often unstated

Few patient or resource outcomes

Finding 1 · inconsistent definitions

Leptomeningeal treatment response is defined inconsistently

Even the most-reported response outcome is defined in many different ways; survival is timed inconsistently; and most outcomes name no assessor.

Reported by 22 of 41 trials; only 6 used a RANO-LM-based standard (5 RANO-LM, 1 modified); the other 16 were study-specific.

· N = number of trials using that definition; definitions without a number were each used by one trial.

Reference

Response-assessment criteria for leptomeningeal disease

These are the response-assessment frameworks behind the leptomeningeal-response definitions used in this review, drawn from published criteria. Study-specific composites combine two or more of these elements in a trial-defined way.

Response-assessment definitions

RANO-LM: Response Assessment in Neuro-Oncology, Leptomeningeal MetastasesComposite response combining a standardized neurological examination, CSF cytology (or flow cytometry in haematological cancers), and contrast-enhanced neuraxis MRI graded on a radiological LM response scorecard. Chamberlain et al., Neuro-Oncology 2017.
Modified RANO-LM (radiographic only)The radiographic (MRI) component of RANO-LM applied on its own, without the neurological-examination or CSF-cytology components.
CSF cytology conversionConversion of cerebrospinal-fluid cytology from positive (malignant cells present) to negative (no malignant or equivocal cells). EANO-ESMO Clinical Practice Guideline, ESMO Open 2023.
RECIST 1.1 (LM as non-measurable disease)Leptomeningeal disease is listed under RECIST 1.1 as truly non-measurable, so it is followed qualitatively as a non-target lesion rather than a measurable target lesion. Eisenhauer et al., 2009.

Transparency

Methods and limitations

This work follows COMET and COS-STAD for core outcome set development, and PRISMA for the systematic review. Outcomes were extracted from published leptomeningeal disease trials, standardised to defined terms and mapped to COMET core areas. This is a descriptive catalogue of reported outcomes, not the final core outcome set.

The ask

An invitation to the Society for Neuro-Oncology

LeptoCOS invites the Society for Neuro-Oncology to support the initiative and to help disseminate the resulting core outcome set to its members. A two-round eDelphi across all stakeholder groups will prioritise this long list, and a consensus meeting will agree the final set; this companion presents the evidence behind that process rather than carrying it out. For a high-mortality, under-served disease, SNO’s support would help turn this shared evidence base into a standard its members and patients can rely on, reflecting the community it serves before the set is fixed.
